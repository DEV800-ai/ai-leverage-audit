# AI Leverage Audit — Intake

A 5–10 minute questionnaire. Free-text answers are fine — be specific to your own work, not generic.

Your responses will go through an AI Operating Mentor that produces a personalised report: a map of your recurring workflows, where AI can save you real time, and one concrete 30-day experiment to try.

Nothing here gets stored anywhere public.

## 2. Your weekly workflows

**What recurring tasks do you do most weeks?** List 5–10. Be specific — "client onboarding emails" beats "comms".
> Example: *"discovery calls with new leads, writing 2–3 proposals/week, monthly invoicing for 6 clients, weekly status updates per project, content for LinkedIn 2x/week, …"*

**What tools do you currently use?** (CRMs, email, calendar, accounting, content, etc.)
> Example: *"Notion, Stripe, ConvertKit, Calendly, Gmail, Figma, …"*

**What eats the most of your time, and feels like it shouldn't?**
> Example: *"Writing proposals from scratch every time — they're structurally similar but I redo 80% manually. Following up on stale leads is something I forget."*

---

## 3. Where AI scares you a little

**What's costly if it goes wrong** (errors damage trust, money, or reputation)?
> Example: *"Anything that goes directly to clients — invoices, proposals, deliverables. Newsletter sends. Public posts."*

**What touches customer relationships directly?**
> Example: *"Discovery calls, all client comms, proposals, design reviews, the newsletter."*

**Are there things you'd refuse to automate, even if AI could?** (Optional)
> Example: *"Direct client conversations. I'm fine if AI helps me draft but I always send the final."*

---

## 4. Measurement baseline (optional but valuable)

Pick the one or two tasks from section 2 that eat the most time. For each, answer what you can — estimates are fine.

**Which task are you measuring?**
>

**How many do you process per week?** (invoices, orders, clients, requests, etc.)
>

**How long does one take you today, start to finish?** (minutes)
>

**Roughly what % of that time is mechanical** (copy-pasting, typing, searching) vs judgment (checking, deciding, approving)?
> Mechanical: ___ % / Judgment: ___ %

**How often do you catch a mistake?** (e.g. "1 in 20 invoices", "rarely", "almost every batch")
>

**If it happens, how long to fix one mistake?** (minutes)
>

**If AI handled the mechanical part, what % of the time would you still want to review the output yourself?**
> Example: *"I'd check every single one"* = 100% / *"spot-check 1 in 5"* = 20%

**What would "this worked" look like after 30 days?** (your words, not a metric)
> Example: *"I stop dreading Monday morning invoicing"* / *"I can process in half the time"*

**What would make you stop using it?**
> Example: *"If I have to fix more than 2 mistakes per batch"* / *"If my staff don't trust the output"*

*(Repeat the block above for a second task if you want.)*

## When you're done

Send your responses back as plain text. We'll convert them to the structured intake the audit uses, run the audit, and share your 30-day bet within ~1 minute.

The audit produces:

- A map of your recurring workflows.
- Per-workflow analysis: where to **automate** / **assist** / **keep human**.
- One concrete 30-day experiment with success and failure metrics.
- Areas to keep human-controlled regardless.
- A first-version playbook to build on.

No commitment beyond the conversation we'll have afterward about whether the 30-day bet feels specific and doable to you.
